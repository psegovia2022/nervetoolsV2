Small Manipulator

This device has been used to record the extracellular activity of single units and field potentials in the prefrontal-hippocampal circuit of behaving mice. We used tetrodes made of 17 um polyimide-coated nichrome wires (AM Systems) to study the interregional oscillatory coupling (slow-gamma: 20 to 40 Hz) during spatial navigational learning (Negr√≥n-Oyarzo et al., 2018).

Additionally, we have recorded single cells activity and field potentials by using tetrodes on behaving rats. The same device was used as the physical platform to hold electrodes to deploy electric micro-stimulation. Both approaches were done using 12 um nichrome wires (California Fine Wire Company) (Aguilar-Rivera et al., 2020).

You can find a detailed description of this manipulator in both publications. If you download and use it, please cite the publication that better matches your research.

We have successfully printed the plastic parts of this device using "smooth fine detail plastic," "white processed versatile," and "black Hi-Def acrylate." All these materials are available for 3D printing at Shapeways website.
